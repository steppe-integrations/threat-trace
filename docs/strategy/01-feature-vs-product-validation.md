# 01 — Feature-vs-product validation

## Purpose

Foundational. If buyers want a teaching/rehearsal layer **bundled with their AI agent vendor**, the standalone product play is dead and the consulting + framework + audit plays become primary. If they want it **standalone and vendor-neutral**, green-light the AML port and the second-vertical motion. Everything else cascades from this answer.

## Key questions

- Would CISOs / CCOs pay for a pre-deployment teaching layer separate from their agent vendor's onboarding?
- If Prophet, Dropzone, or Torq ships a "rehearsal mode" inside their product, would buyers switch back to a third-party tool?
- Which budget line funds it — L&D, security training, compliance program, professional services, procurement?
- What's the *evaluation criteria* — depth of content, vendor-neutrality, regulator-acceptance, integration?

## Who to talk to

Target 5–7 conversations, mix of buyer states:
- 2 CISOs at firms **already piloting** Prophet / Dropzone / Torq
- 2 CISOs **not yet evaluating** AI SOC vendors
- 1 BSA Officer or Compliance lead at a regional bank (cross-vertical sanity check)
- 1 head of security awareness/training at a F500 (the L&D budget angle)
- 1 ex-buyer who *evaluated* an AI agent and chose not to deploy (highest signal — what was missing?)

Warm intros via Steppe network beat cold outbound 10:1. LinkedIn second-degree search "CISO" + "AI agent."

## Talking prompts

Use these as conversation starters, not a script. 15-min calls.

- **Opener**: "I'm working on a tool that lets your team experience what an AI security agent will do — clicking through alerts the way the agent would, with full traceability of its reasoning — *before* you deploy. I'm trying to figure out whether that's a thing you'd want from a third party, or whether you'd expect it bundled with whichever agent vendor you pick."
- **Bundling probe**: "If [Prophet / Dropzone] shipped a 'demo mode' tomorrow that did the same thing, would you still want a vendor-neutral version? Why?"
- **Budget**: "If you bought it, what budget line would it come from? Who would have to sign?"
- **Falsification**: "What would have to be true for you to *not* buy this?"
- **Regulator angle**: "Would you want this if your auditor / board explicitly asked you to demonstrate AI competency?"

## Decision tree

| Outcome | Implication |
|---|---|
| 4+/5 say "we'd want it bundled" | Kill standalone product play. Pivot weight to brief 06 (OSS framework) + brief 10 (audit). The teaching pattern becomes a *consulting* and *open framework* play, not a SaaS. |
| 4+/5 say "we'd want it standalone, vendor-neutral" | Green-light AML port (brief 02) as proof #2. Standalone product is viable. |
| Split 3/2 | Look for the persona difference — regulated vs not, mid-market vs enterprise, security vs AML. There may be a real segment, just not "all CISOs." |
| Nobody can articulate budget | Revenue model is a problem regardless of bundling — needs more discovery before any build. |

## Starter steps (this week)

1. Build the 30-person warm-intro list from network (CISOs, CCOs, security training leads).
2. Draft the 6-question script (single page, ~15 min).
3. Send 10 personalized intro asks. Aim for 5 booked calls in 14 days.
4. Set up a one-page synthesis doc — fill it in *after each call*, not at the end.
5. Prep the falsification version of the pitch — describe the *bundled* alternative honestly so buyers can react to it.

## Risks / caveats

- **Confirmation bias** is the killer. Script for falsification; reward the buyer for telling you it's a bad idea.
- "Interesting" is not a buying signal. Listen for **budget specificity**, **timeline urgency**, and **named owner** — anything else is noise.
- Sample bias toward people willing to talk to founders. Try to get at least 1 buyer who pushes back hard.
- Bundled-vs-standalone may be the wrong frame entirely if buyers conceive of this as **professional services** rather than **software**. Listen for that reframe.
