# 02 — AML buyer-channel research

## Purpose

In financial services, multiple personas could buy a rehearsal/competency tool for AML AI agents — and each implies a different sale, price point, and product shape. Wrong persona = wrong wedge, slow procurement, no renewal. Need to identify the actual budget owner before pitching.

## Key questions

- Among BSA Officer, CCO, Head of Internal Audit, Head of L&D — who actually owns budget for AI competency / training artifacts?
- Is this a procurement-led purchase (RFP, security review, 9–18 month cycle) or champion-led (contained budget, fast)?
- What does a typical "compliance training" or "AI governance" line item look like at an FI — annual, per-seat, per-cert?
- How does FinCEN's 2026 proposed rule (comments closed June 9, 2026) shift accountability for AI governance? Has it finalized?
- Do examiners (OCC, FRB, FDIC, NYDFS) currently ask for "AI competency artifacts," or only "model risk documentation"?

## Who to talk to

- 2 BSA Officers — one large bank ($100B+ assets), one community bank
- 1 Chief Compliance Officer at a regional bank
- 1 Head of Internal Audit at a large FI
- 1 product / partnerships person at Unit21, Lucinity, Moody's, or Napier (vendor-side view of how their buyers think)
- 1 ex-OCC or ex-FRB examiner (what regulators actually want to see)
- 1 ACAMS chapter board member (community signal)

## Talking prompts

- **Ownership**: "If your bank deployed an agentic AML system tomorrow, who owns 'are our analysts trained to evaluate what the agent produces'? Where does that report?"
- **Examiner**: "When examiners ask about AI governance under [FinCEN proposed rule / SR 11-7], what artifacts do they want to see? What have you been asked for that you couldn't provide?"
- **Budget shape**: "What does a typical 'AML training' or 'compliance enablement' line item look like — annual contract, per-seat, per-cert?"
- **Failure mode**: "If you bought a tool like this and your team didn't use it, why would that be?"
- **Vendor lens (to Unit21/Lucinity)**: "When your customers buy your platform, what training / change-management asks come up? Who owns that internally?"

## Decision tree

| If buyer is… | Sell as… | Pricing shape | Likely contract |
|---|---|---|---|
| BSA Officer | "Examiner-ready competency artifact" | Per-analyst seat or per-program | Annual, $40–150K |
| CCO | "AI governance program component" | Tiered by program scope | Annual, $100–500K |
| Internal Audit | "Third-party validation of vendor AI claims" — overlaps with brief 10 | Per-engagement or annual | $75–300K |
| L&D | Training content | Per-seat | $20–100K, lowest leverage |
| Procurement-led | Slow, high-friction, RFP. Probably not the first sale. | — | — |

If multiple personas have *partial* claims, the real buyer is whoever the regulator or auditor talks to. That's usually CCO or BSA Officer.

## Starter steps (this week)

1. LinkedIn search "BSA Officer" + "AI" — find who is publicly speaking/posting on agentic AML. They're already pre-sold on the problem.
2. Read 5 public comment letters submitted to FinCEN's 2026 proposed rule (FinCEN publishes them) — extract the buyer-side language. Note who signed.
3. Reach out to one vendor-side partnerships person (Unit21 publishes accessible blog content; partnerships email is on their site).
4. Find the next ACAMS chapter event in your region — register, walk in with a clear ask.
5. Build a 1-page "AML buyer landscape" sketch from research and update after each call.

## Risks / caveats

- AML community is **culturally conservative**. "AI vendor" framing may turn off buyers — frame as "compliance enablement" or "examiner-readiness."
- Bank procurement cycles are **9–18 months**. Early conversations don't translate to revenue fast. Plan accordingly — design partner motion (brief 09) is the way to compress this.
- FinCEN rule may not finalize on schedule, or may finalize weaker than expected. Don't make the entire thesis dependent on it.
- Multiple personas with *partial* ownership is the norm; the sale becomes coalition-building, not single-champion. Plan for 2–3 internal stakeholders per deal.
- Regional and community banks have *very* different decision dynamics from tier-1; don't generalize across.
