# 10 — Third-party agent audit market

## Purpose

**New thread from the user.** Distinct from the rehearsal/teaching play. Premise: every AI agent vendor will claim to "show their work." But who validates that claim? An **independent, cross-vendor, traceability-first audit framework** would meet a real and growing regulatory demand (FinCEN, NAIC, EU AI Act) — and structurally **sidesteps the feature-vs-product risk** because the AI vendor cannot credibly audit themselves.

This may be the most defensible single play in the portfolio.

## Strategic distinctions vs the teaching play

| | Teaching (rehearsal) | Audit |
|---|---|---|
| When | Pre-deployment | Post-deployment, ongoing |
| Buyer | AI buyer (CISO, CCO) | AI buyer's stakeholders (board, regulator, internal audit, insurer) |
| Sells trust to | The user of the agent | Everyone *outside* the user |
| Feature-vs-product risk | High — vendors can build this in | **Low** — vendors *can't* audit themselves |
| Underlying tech | traceability + expectation framework | Same |
| Founder-leverage fit | Medium | **High** (knowledge + connections + trusted-third-party brand) |

The same engineering pattern serves both. The product, buyer, motion, and pricing are fundamentally different.

## Key questions

- Is there demand **today** for third-party AI agent audit, or is it a 12–24 month bet?
- Who are the buyers — internal audit, compliance, risk, the board, regulators directly, insurers?
- What's the audit deliverable — a one-shot report, continuous monitoring product, certification mark, or all three?
- Who currently does this — Big 4, AI-specific firms (BABL AI, Eticas, Holistic AI), or nobody?
- Is this best as a **software product** (SaaS for internal audit), a **consulting practice with software underneath**, or a **framework licensed to Big 4**?

## Existing players to study

- **Big 4 AI assurance practices**: PwC "Responsible AI"; Deloitte "Trustworthy AI"; EY and KPMG similar. All consulting-led; tooling underneath is generally weak. Opportunity to be the tooling.
- **BABL AI** — independent AI audit firm, NYC Local Law 144 audits ([babl.ai](https://babl.ai))
- **Eticas Foundation / Eticas.ai** — algorithmic audit consultancy
- **Holistic AI** — AI governance + audit platform; closest direct competitor on the platform side
- **Credo AI** — AI governance / policy platform — adjacent but not audit
- **Anthropic, OpenAI** — publish system cards / safety evals for their own models — internal, not third-party
- **NIST AI RMF** — voluntary framework but increasingly cited; map our framework to it for credibility

## Regulatory tailwinds (already in motion)

- **EU AI Act** (in force Aug 2024; high-risk obligations from Aug 2026) — third-party conformity assessment for high-risk AI
- **NYC Local Law 144** — bias audits for hiring AI, live since 2023; the model others are following
- **FinCEN proposed rule (2026)** — implies expectation of independent validation
- **NAIC Model Bulletin on AI** — adopted in 23 states + DC; mandates explainability, audit trails, human review for adverse decisions, bias testing
- **SR 11-7** (Federal Reserve model risk guidance) — extends to AI models in regulated FIs
- **Colorado AI Act** (effective Feb 2026) — algorithmic discrimination disclosures
- **California SB 1047 / successors** — pending, but signals direction

## Decision tree

| Demand validation | Implication |
|---|---|
| **Demand validated today** | Audit framework + consulting practice could be a separate product line, OR the *primary* line if teaching is too crowded. Build alongside teaching, not after. |
| **Demand still nascent** | Position as "audit-ready by design" feature of the rehearsal product, harvest later when regulation matures |
| **Demand is captured by Big 4** | Partner — white-label the framework as their tooling — rather than compete head-on |
| **Demand exists but buyers don't know what to ask for** | Big educational play; lean into thought leadership (briefs 06, 09); become the trusted voice |

## Risks / caveats

- **Regulatory pace is uncertain.** FinCEN may not finalize as expected; EU enforcement may be soft; US federal AI legislation is volatile. Don't bet the entire thesis on a single rule landing on time.
- **"Audit" framing is heavier than "rehearsal"** — longer sales cycle, more procurement, more legal review, more skeptical buyers. Plan for 6–12 months to revenue.
- **Competing with Big 4 head-on is brutal** — they own the buyer relationships in regulated industries. Partnership is more realistic than direct competition.
- **Audit credibility requires deep domain expertise per vertical.** Steppe needs to either hire it (slow, expensive) or partner for it (faster, dilutive). Plan accordingly.
- **Independence is sacred for audit.** If we also sell the rehearsal product to the same buyers, that's a perceived conflict. Either keep them as separate brands/legal entities, or pick one.
- **Audit is a credentialed market.** "Who is Steppe to audit our AI?" is a fair question. Building credibility takes time — through publications, framework adoption (brief 06), and design partner case studies (brief 09).
